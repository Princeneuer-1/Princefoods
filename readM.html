Kenkey - Traditional Ghanaian Dish

Kenkey is a traditional staple dish in Ghana, made from fermented maize or corn dough. It is popular among the Ga and Fante people in Ghana, but it is enjoyed by many across the country and beyond.

How Kenkey is Made

The process of making Kenkey involves:

  • Fermenting maize or corn dough for 2-3 days.
  • Mixing the fermented dough with fresh corn dough to reduce its sourness.
  • Cooking a portion of the dough until it's partially cooked (called "aflata").
  • Shaping the dough into balls and wrapping them in corn husks or plantain leaves.
  • Steaming the wrapped dough for 2-3 hours until fully cooked.

Serving Kenkey

Kenkey is typically served with:

  • Fried fish or grilled fish.
  • Shito (spicy black pepper sauce).
  • Freshly ground pepper, onions, and tomatoes.

This hearty meal is enjoyed for its unique taste, nutritional value, and the cultural significance it holds in Ghanaian communities.
